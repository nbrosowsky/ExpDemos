Instructions:

On each trial three images will appear in either blue, green, red or yellow. Your task is to identify the color of the center image as quickly as possible using the keyboard.

For example:

You will press "b" if the center image is blue, "g" if the center image is green, "r" if the center image is red, and "y" if the center image is yellow.

Once you make a response you will automatically proceed to the next trial.

After every 80 trials you will receive a break screen. Feel free to take a short break.

Experimental Debriefing

Thank you for your participation in our experiment.

This document serves as short description of the purposes behind our experiment.

You just completed what is commonly known as a Flanker task. On each trial you were presented with three images. Sometimes all three images were in the same color (congruent) and sometimes they were presented in different colors (incongruent). Typically people are slower to respond when the color of the center image is different from the flanking images demonstrating an inability to ignore the conflicting information. The current experiment investigated whether repeating specific images with either the same or different color configuration (congruent versus incongruent) would change this effect.

We are conducting this study to investigate these spatial aspects of human attention across a wide range of participants.
